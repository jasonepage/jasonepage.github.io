Hearken

Keep their stories — privately.

A private iPhone app for recording an elder's spoken stories and turning them — entirely on the device — into a clear, readable narrative and searchable records. Built for cultural preservation, with the speaker always in control.

Early build · in TestFlight

The story stays the speaker's

The most important thing to know: Hearken never invents. Everything the app produces — the narrative, the names, the dates — is an editable draft the speaker confirms, drawn only from what was actually said. It preserves the speaker's own voice and flags anything uncertain rather than guessing.

What it does

Just talk

A single large Record button, or a gentle one-question-at-a-time interview. The elder simply tells their story, at their own pace.

On-device transcription

Recordings become an editable transcript right on the phone — tap a part to hear that moment of the recording again.

A refined narrative

The transcript becomes a readable story in the speaker's words, in a style you choose — "as told," concise, or for grandchildren.

Searchable records

People, places, events, dates, service, community participation, and cultural terms — each surfaced as a suggestion to confirm, never an asserted fact.

Photo stories

Add a photograph from the family album and describe it aloud; the description is transcribed and kept with the picture.

Archival export

Export a story as a readable document, structured data, or a draft "museum package" — only after consent, and with nothing marked private.

Private & offline

Hearken runs on-device using Apple's on-device intelligence and on-device transcription, so it works in airplane mode and nothing ever leaves the phone. There are no accounts, no ads, no tracking, and no servers. Before anything can be exported, the app records consent — and any record, sentence, photo, or whole story can be marked Restricted and is then removed from every export.

An honest note

Hearken is built by an enrolled member of the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde, for cultural preservation under tribal control. The app works end-to-end, but the interview questions and the "museum package" archival fields are neutral placeholders pending review by Grand Ronde Cultural Resources / Lifeways staff. No sacred or tribe-specific content is authored by the app, and the community stays in control of how stories are captured, kept, and shared.
