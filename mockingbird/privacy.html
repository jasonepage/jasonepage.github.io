Mockingbird — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Mockingbird is built to be private by design. The app has no accounts, no logins, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking, and we operate no servers that receive your data.

On-device analysis

Mockingbird scores your pronunciation by analyzing the sound of your voice. This core analysis runs entirely on your device. Your scores, stars, streaks, and settings are stored only on your device and are removed if you delete the app.

Microphone

The microphone is used only while you are actively practicing, to measure your speech. Recordings are processed for scoring and are not stored by the app beyond what is needed to score the current attempt.

Optional AI coach

Mockingbird includes an optional “AI coach” that gives more precise feedback. It is off by default. If you choose to enable it and provide your own API key, the recording for that attempt is sent to Google's Gemini service to be scored and is then discarded. That feature is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. Any API key you enter is stored only on your device. The AI coach is never used in the kids' picture-word mode.

Children

The picture-word mode for young learners is always processed 100% on the device — a child's audio never leaves the phone and is never sent to any third party. Mockingbird does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

What we collect

Nothing is collected by us. We have no servers and receive no data. Because Mockingbird collects nothing on our end, there is no personal data to request, export, or delete from us.
