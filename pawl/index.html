Why Pawl is different

Every other blocker fails for the same reason: you can turn it off the moment you crave it. A passcode you set is a passcode you can remove. Pawl gates unblocking behind a physical USB-C security key you put out of reach, plus a 15-minute cooling-off timer. No key in hand, no unblock. The urge passes; the block holds.

How it works

  1. Shield the temptation. Choose the sportsbook, casino, betting, and crypto apps and sites you want gone. Pawl blocks them with Apple's Screen Time and shows a calm "Locked by Pawl" screen — with no unlock button on it.
  2. Lock it behind a key you can't reach. Lifting the shield requires a physical FIDO2 security key. Put it in a drawer at a friend's place, a locked box — anywhere that isn't your pocket at midnight.
  3. Cool off before anything changes. Even with the key, unblocking starts a 15-minute timer before the shield lifts — then it relocks automatically.

Honest about the limits

Pawl is friction, not a cage. You could still delete the app or turn off Screen Time — and Pawl tells you so plainly rather than pretending otherwise. The point is to make the impulsive choice a deliberate one. For most people, putting a physical key out of reach is the difference that finally sticks.
