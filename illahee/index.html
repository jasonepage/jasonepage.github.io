Illahee

Learn Chinuk Wawa, the safe way.

A free iPhone and iPad app for learning Chinuk Wawa, the heritage language of the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde — built around elders' voices, patient practice, and a living land that grows as you learn.

Prototype · in TestFlight

Safe by design

The most important thing to know: the AI can never invent, translate, or score Chinuk Wawa. Every Chinuk Wawa word and recording comes only from verified content the Language Program approves, and a safety layer blocks anything that isn't from that trusted source. The AI helps only on the English side — coaching and encouraging — never producing the language itself.

What it does

Guided lessons

Short, ordered lessons with listen-and-choose, build-a-phrase, say-it, and recall — all from verified words.

A patient coach

When you miss an answer, an on-device coach quietly re-explains it in plain English. It stays out of the way otherwise.

Elders' voices

A hands-free listening mode plays real elder recordings so you can practice on a walk or while doing chores.

Your living land

A seasonal landscape grows as you learn — trees for words, a river for your streak, mountains for lessons.

Name the world

Point your camera at something; the app names it in English and shows the verified Chinuk Wawa word, if one exists.

Sounds guide

The distinctive sounds of Chinuk Wawa, explained plainly, with example words and record-and-compare practice.

Picture-based learning

Simple on-device illustrations of a word's English meaning help it stick — no reading required.

Family & classroom

Several learners can share one device, each with their own land and progress. No accounts, kid-safe.

Private & offline

Illahee uses on-device Apple Intelligence, so it works offline and nothing leaves your phone. There are no accounts, no ads, and no tracking. Your progress stays private in your own iCloud.

An honest note

Illahee is an early prototype. The app's engine is built and working, but it currently runs on clearly-labeled placeholder words — not real Chinuk Wawa. Real words and elder voices depend on a partnership and data-sovereignty agreement with the Grand Ronde Chinuk Wawa Language Program. The language belongs to the community, and the community stays in control of how it is used and shared.
