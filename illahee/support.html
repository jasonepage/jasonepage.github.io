Illahee — Support

How can we help?

Illahee is a free app for learning Chinuk Wawa, the heritage language of the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde. Here are answers to common questions. For anything else, email natepage67@gmail.com and you'll get a reply.

Getting started

Open the app, set up a learner profile, and start on the Learn tab. Tap a lesson to begin listening, choosing, and practicing words. Your progress grows the "land" on the Progress tab as you go.

Is this real Chinuk Wawa?

Not yet. This early version runs on clearly-labeled placeholder words so the experience can be tested. Real, verified words and elder recordings depend on a partnership with the Grand Ronde Chinuk Wawa Language Program. Until that content is in place, please don't treat the words in the app as Chinuk Wawa to learn from.

Does the AI make up words?

No — by design it cannot. The AI only ever helps in English (gentle coaching after a wrong answer). Every Chinuk Wawa word and recording comes from verified content, and a safety layer blocks anything that isn't from that source. The app never generates, translates, or scores Chinuk Wawa.

Why does it want my microphone or camera?

The microphone is used only when you record yourself to play your own attempt back; the camera is used only for the "name the world" feature, where on-device vision names an object in English. Both work on your device and nothing is uploaded. You can change these permissions any time in the iOS Settings app under Illahee.

Does it work offline? Is my data private?

Yes. Illahee runs on-device and works offline. There are no accounts, ads, or tracking, and your progress stays private on your device and in your own iCloud. See the Privacy Policy for details.

I'm with the Language Program

Illahee is built to be a tool for the community, with the community in control of its language. If you're with the Grand Ronde Chinuk Wawa Language Program and want to talk about verified content, elder recordings, or data sovereignty, please reach out directly.
