Seal — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Seal is a private messenger built so that no one — including us — can read your messages. We operate no servers and collect nothing about you.

What we never collect

  • No phone number, email address, or contact-list upload. Seal accounts are cryptographic keys, not personal identifiers.
  • No analytics, no advertising, no tracking, no data sales — architecturally impossible and permanently off the table.

End-to-end encryption

All messages and photos are end-to-end encrypted on your device before they are stored or transmitted. They sync through Apple's iCloud (CloudKit) as ciphertext. Neither we nor Apple can decrypt them. Encryption keys live in your device's Secure Enclave and keychain and never leave your device.

What is visible, honestly stated

  • Public directory: your chosen display name, your public keys, and your identity tier are published to a public directory so friends can verify you. Use any name you like — it doesn't have to be your real one.
  • Metadata: Apple's infrastructure can observe routing metadata (which identities exchange messages, and when). Message content is never visible.
  • Disappearing messages are deleted by the recipients' apps at expiry. Like every messenger, this is best-effort: a recipient could screenshot a message before it expires.

Your data, your control

  • Chats, friendships, and keys are stored locally on your device, encrypted by iOS.
  • Signing out wipes all local data for that identity. Deleting the app removes everything on the device.
  • Because we hold no data about you, there is nothing for us to export, sell, or hand over.
