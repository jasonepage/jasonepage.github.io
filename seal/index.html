Friendships are forged, not followed

There are no usernames to search and no contact lists to upload. To become friends in Seal, you meet in person: your friend taps their hardware security key — or uses their passkey — on your phone, cryptographically signing the moment. Bots can mint a million accounts. They can't stand in a room with you.

Sealed shut

Every message and photo is end-to-end encrypted before it leaves your device, with keys held in your iPhone's Secure Enclave. We run zero servers and can read nothing — there's no honeypot, because there's no pot.

No phone numbers. No ads. No tracking. No data to sell, ever.

Two rings, one rule

● Brass — Verified

Identity rooted in a physical FIDO2 hardware key. The strongest proof of personhood available on a phone.

● Silver — Passkey

Face ID passkey identity. Thirty seconds to join, the same encryption, the same in-person ceremonies.

Your forge log

Every friendship you forge is a signed, timestamped proof that you met a human. Over the years it becomes something no other app can give you: a verifiable diary of your real-world social life.
