Reporting objectionable content

Every message from another person can be reported. Press and hold the message and tap Report. The reported message and the sender are sent to our moderation contact for review, and the sender is blocked on your device at the same time. You can also email jasonepage@gmail.com to report abuse or a user.

Blocking abusive users

You can block any other person at any time. Press and hold one of their messages and tap Block, or open the verification panel from the chat header and toggle Block next to their name. Blocked users are hidden from your chats and cannot reach you. Blocking is reversible.

Our moderation commitment

We review every report and act on objectionable content and abusive users by removing the offending user within 24 hours of the report. Enforcement includes permanently removing ("tombstoning") the reported identity from the public directory, after which other people can no longer verify or reach that identity.

How moderation works with end-to-end encryption. Seal cannot read your messages — they are end-to-end encrypted and we operate no servers that can decrypt them. Moderation is therefore report-driven: when you report a message, your device includes the specific reported content in the report so a human can review it. We never have visibility into conversations that are not reported to us.

Identity and security

Your Seal identity is rooted in a hardware security key or a passkey on your device. You are responsible for keeping your keys safe. If you lose all of your registered keys, your identity cannot be recovered — by design, there is no password, email, or SMS reset.

Account deletion

You can permanently delete your identity at any time from the You tab → Delete identity. This removes your identity record from the public directory and wipes all local data on your device. Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.
